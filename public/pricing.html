Valuation Framework

This section is intentionally illustrative. Replace assumptions with live deals and actual ARR before using it externally.

Simple valuation math

For a software platform with infrastructure-like retention and enterprise security posture, the practical shorthand is revenue multiple logic:

Valuation = Annual Recurring Revenue × Revenue Multiple

The multiple depends on proof. Higher multiples require repeatable sales, low churn, defensible security features, and evidence that the platform becomes sticky operational infrastructure.

Why the multiple can be stronger than a thin AI wrapper

  • There is already a separated policy gate instead of raw browser-to-model traffic.
  • Tokens have scope, TTL, and email lock constraints, which are governance primitives.
  • Operational surfaces like SkyeMail, SkyeChat, SkyeDrive, and SkyDex create workflow gravity.
  • Smoke evidence and audit artifacts reduce the “demo-only” discount buyers usually apply.

Do not say “the company is worth X” unless you have live financing or executed commercial data supporting that. Say: “Here is the valuation framework based on ARR scenarios and enterprise software multiples.” That keeps the page usable in diligence without overstating facts.

Cross-App Workflow Spine

This is the product lane the pricing has to justify: one command belt where work moves across apps instead of dying inside isolated tools.

Authoring lane

Draft in SkyeDocxPro, persist to SkyeDrive, move into SkyeBookX for expansion, AI continuation, and art generation, then export as PDF or encrypted .skye packages.

Publishing lane

Move the output into SkyeBlog, post it to SkyeChat, syndicate through SkyeMail, and keep the whole path under the same auth and policy gate instead of copying between unrelated products.

Engineering lane

Use SkyDex to pull workspace material from SkyeDrive, stage diffs, manage restore points, connect to Git when needed, and keep SKNore-protected content out of push/export paths.

Truth requirement

The architecture only earns premium pricing if every hop tells the truth. Wrong PIN should say wrong PIN. Blocked mail ingest should say blocked mail ingest. Failed smoke should stay red. Silent failure destroys both investor confidence and operator trust.

Technical Positioning

The pricing only holds if the architecture reads like a durable system, not a thin front end over a model API.

Client Plane

Replaceable browser surfaces including the main Vite shell, standalone apps, and marketing or procurement pages.

Policy Plane

Netlify Functions enforce session, tenancy, token rules, gateway routing, and auditable action boundaries.

Execution Plane

Cloudflare Worker handles privileged operations and secure backend flows instead of exposing those paths to the browser.

Data Plane

Neon/Postgres plus KV and R2 style storage patterns support workspace, audit, record, and evidence persistence.

Technical moat bullets you can actually defend

  • Gateway-only AI routing means provider keys stay server-side.
  • Scoped and locked tokens support controlled machine and workflow access.
  • SKNore gives a policy story around protected payload boundaries.
  • Cross-app surfaces create a suite narrative instead of isolated utilities.
  • Smoke evidence and operational artifacts support enterprise review conversations.

Where premium pricing comes from technically

Premium pricing is justified when the buyer is not only purchasing output generation. They are purchasing managed execution paths, secure access patterns, auditability, deployment discipline, and the ability to standardize multiple workflows inside one governed surface. That is why the suite should be sold like an operating layer for work, not a prompt box.
